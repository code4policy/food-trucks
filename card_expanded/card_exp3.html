Get a Business Certificate

Boston businesses must obtain a certificate from the City Clerk’s Office, with renewal every four years

📄 Make sure to collect

  • Health Permit
  • Fire Permit
  • Hawkers and Peddlers License
  • Commissary Kitchen Agreement or Letter
  • Certificate of Liability Insurance
    • ✅

    Be prepared to:

    • Provide the name and address of your business.
    • Include the names and addresses of any individuals with an interest in your business.
    • Submit a copy of your lease agreement, including:
      • Signatures
      • Start and end dates
    • If you do not have a lease, provide a notarized letter from your landlord confirming that your business is a tenant.
    ✅

    Print and complete a business certificate form

    • Make sure to check the box that says "new filing" if applicable
    • The business owner's signature must be notarized, which can be done at the City Clerk's Office with a valid photo ID
    ✅

    Collect certificate in person or mail in documents

      Bring your documents, payment, and completed form to City Hall:

      City Clerk’s Office, Room 601

      1 City Hall Square, Boston, MA 02201

      Hours: Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

      OR

      Mail us your info

      Be sure to include a self-addressed, stamped envelope with your letter. Mail your documents, payment, and completed form to:

      City Clerk’s Office, Room 601

      1 City Hall Square, Boston, MA 02201

      Hours: Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

    💲 Fees

    The filing fee is $65. If you are not a Massachusetts resident but you want to conduct business in the state, you need to pay an additional $35.
