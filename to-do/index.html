Starting a Food Truck in Boston

Your Food Truck Business is Just Steps Away

Like any other business, you need to get the permits from the relevant officials, register your business, and have a juicy location for your food truck.

📄 Obtain Permits and Documents

These are the 6 permits and documents that you need

Required Documents

  • a Health and Fire Permits
  • a Hawker/Peddler License Form
  • a Business Certificate Form
  • a Certificate of Liability Insurance
  • a Food Truck Business Plan
  • a A Written Agreement from your Commissary

    • Health and Fire Permit

    Before building food truck

    1. Schedule a review of building plan

      2. You should go over the plans for your truck with Inspectional Services before you start building. You can schedule an appointment to review your construction and operation plans by calling Tom McAdams at 617-961-3293.

    2. Bring the necessary documents to the review
      • equipment plans and specifications for your truck
      • a food truck plan review form to find out what other permits you’ll need
      • a completed health permit application
      • a sample menu to help you figure out the layout of the kitchen (Our recommendation: You will need a sample menu again when writing up your business plan. It is a good idea to meet those requirements now so you do not have to write it again later)

      💰$100 annual fee for the health permit
